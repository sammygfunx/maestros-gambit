How to Play

Maestro's Gambit is classical chess performed by two rival orchestras. Standard chess rules apply — castling, en passant, promotion, the lot.

ViolinistPawnNimble section player. Reaches the far end of the stage and earns a promotion.
CellistKnightRides the cello in sweeping L-shaped glissandos, leaping over the section.
ClarinetistBishopGlides along the diagonals with piercing runs.
PercussionistRookA one-man battery. Thunders straight down ranks and files.
PianistQueenThe virtuoso. Strikes in any direction — and travels with the piano.
ConductorKingLoses the conductor and the concert is over. Protect the podium.

Click a musician to see their legal moves, then click a highlighted square. Capturing triggers an on-stage duel. Outcomes are decided by the rules of chess — the show is purely for the audience.

Play a friend online: on the setup screen choose Online Duel, then Host a Match to get a room code (send it to your friend) or Join with a code they sent you. The match begins the moment they connect — no accounts, no setup. Battle scenes stay a personal preference, so each of you can toggle them freely.
