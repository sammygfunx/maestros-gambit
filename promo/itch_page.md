## About this game (long description)

**Two orchestras. One board. Every capture, a performance.**

The Ivory Sinfonia and the Ebony Philharmonic settle their rivalry the only
way maestros can — over the chessboard, on a concert stage. But this is no quiet
match: the instant one musician takes another, the camera dives to the stage and the
two fight it out in a fully animated duel. A pianist drops a grand piano on a
conductor. A percussionist unleashes a mallet shockwave. A violinist lunges with a
bow held like a rapier. Checkmate gets a full curtain-call finale.

Underneath the show is **a complete, honest chess game** — full rules, a real engine,
and opponents that genuinely range from "hangs pieces" to "will punish your mistakes."

### Features
- **36 hand-animated capture duels**, most with two or three alternate takes, so a
  repeated matchup rarely plays out the same way twice — plus special set-pieces for
  castling, en passant, promotion ("A Star Is Born"), and an elaborate checkmate finale.
- **Solo Recital** against a rated ladder of ten orchestra-themed conductors (~820 to
  ~2010 Elo). Weak ones really do blunder; strong ones open from a real opening book and
  search with a transposition-table engine. No fake difficulty.
- **Career Ladder** — climb the roster band by band, unlocking the next rival as you win.
  (A *Free Play / All Unlocked* toggle is always there for casual players.)
- **Puzzles** — curated mate-in-1/2/3 and win-the-material tactics; land the winning
  move and the stage performs your finishing blow.
- **Duet** (two players, one screen) and **Online Duel** (two players, two machines,
  paired by a short room code — no account, no install).
- **Four board views**, including a clean **Classic 2D** top-down diagram board for when
  you just want to read the position.
- **Built-in trailer reel** — "Watch the Overture" plays a music-synced montage of the
  best duels.
- **Original synth soundtrack** and live-synthesized sound effects — *or load your own
  music*. **PGN export/import**, player profiles with **Elo / Glicko-2** ratings, chess
  clocks, undo, and a **colour-blind-safe board theme** + **Reduce Motion** option.

### Controls
Click (or tap) one of your musicians, then a highlighted square to move. That's it.
A ring marker = a capture; a note marker = a quiet move. Battle scenes are skippable
(click / Esc), or switch to **Quick Captures** to resolve them instantly.

### Made of pure code
Everything you see and hear — every sprite, animation, and note — is generated
procedurally at runtime. No samples, no stock art, no third-party assets (only the
SIL-OFL **Cinzel** display font for the wordmark). It runs entirely in your browser,
offline, with nothing to install.

> *Tip: new to chess? Start with the easiest conductor in Solo Recital, or warm up in
> Puzzles — and read **How to Play** from the title menu.*
