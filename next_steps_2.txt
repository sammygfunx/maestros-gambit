THE HONEST STRATEGIC PICTURE  (read this first)
==================================================================
Maestro's Gambit is, as SOFTWARE, basically done. Full chess rules, a rated
10-persona ladder with an opening book + transposition table, a Career climb,
Puzzles, PGN, online duel + relay, profiles + Elo/Glicko-2, accessibility, a
custom font, four board views, full battle choreography, a synth soundtrack, a
recorded trailer (promo/trailer_reel.mp4) AND a deployable web build
(build/maestros-gambit-web.zip). That is further than most "I'll sell a little
game" projects ever get.

So the bottleneck to "a few bites on itch / Steam / App Store" is NOT more engine
features. In a congested market the things that actually move a $0.99–$1.99 game:
   1. It has to be PURCHASABLE somewhere (packaging + a store page). Right now it
      isn't on any store. This is the single biggest gap.
   2. A non-chess-expert who lands on the page has to instantly "get it" — the
      SPECTACLE (the animated orchestra duels) is the hook, not the chess. The
      page/trailer/first-run must sell the spectacle, and the game must onboard a
      casual buyer who hasn't touched chess since school.
   3. ONE thing that makes a reviewer/streamer say a sentence about it — a
      differentiator beyond "Battle Chess clone." That's the Story/Campaign hook.

So this roadmap is front-loaded with SHIP + MARKET + ONBOARD, then a
differentiating hook, then depth/credibility, then mobile. Engine richness and
extra content matter, but they come AFTER the game is actually for sale.

WHAT I (the AI) CAN AND CAN'T DO HERE — be realistic:
  CAN:   prep/verify builds, write all store + press copy, generate framed stills
         & GIF frames via the ?shot=/?reel= hooks, build in-game onboarding,
         write packaging scaffolds (Electron/Tauri/Capacitor configs, Steamworks
         glue, manifests, icons-from-canvas), design a campaign, strengthen the
         engine, add modes.
  CAN'T: create your itch/Steam/Apple accounts, pay the Steam Direct $100 or Apple
         $99/yr, click "publish", record screen video, or sign/notarize native
         builds on your machine. Those stay yours — I'll get everything to the
         one-click-from-live state and hand you a checklist.

EXECUTION ORDER  (do-first at the top; tiers are loose, reorder freely)
==================================================================
TIER A — GET IT LIVE (lowest effort, highest leverage; starts the feedback loop)
  [ ] A1  Ship to itch.io (web build hygiene + store page + screenshots/GIFs)  medium
  [ ] A2  First-run onboarding / "How to Play" for casual buyers ............. medium
  [ ] A3  Storefront art + press kit (capsule/key art, icon, GIF set, copy) .. medium

TIER B — STEAM (the real revenue path; bigger lift, $100 Steam Direct is yours)
  [ ] B1  Desktop wrapper (Tauri/Electron) + Steamworks (achievements, cloud) . high
  [ ] B2  Steam store page + wishlist/launch campaign copy .................... medium

TIER C — DIFFERENTIATE & DEEPEN (give reviewers a sentence; add replay value)
  [ ] C1  Campaign / Story mode over the Career ladder (THE hook) ............ high
  [ ] C2  Richer evaluation engine pass (honest >2000 rungs, chess cred) ..... high/max
  [ ] C3  Retention content: Daily Puzzle + streak, loadable puzzle book ..... medium
  [ ] C4  Chess960 / fun variants (FEN loader already exists) ................ medium
  [ ] C5  More spectacle: boss duels + 2–3 more soundtrack tracks ............ medium

TIER D — MOBILE (largest market, most packaging friction)
  [ ] D1  Capacitor iOS/Android wrapper + App Store / Play packaging ......... high

CARRIED-FORWARD VALIDATIONS (playtest only — no prompt, just play & note bugs)
  [ ] V1  Real-network + real-device ONLINE test (deploy relay to Render; play
          across two networks/devices; confirm wss:// + free-tier cold start).
  [ ] V2  Real MOBILE pass on actual iOS/Android (safe-area, WebAudio unlock on
          first touch, low-end battle frame rate, pinch-zoom suppression).

WHY THIS ORDER
  - You can't get bites on something nobody can buy. A1 makes it purchasable in
    days, on the friendliest store (itch: HTML5 runs in the browser, no native
    build, pay-what-you-want or $1.99). That also gives you REAL feedback before
    you spend on Steam/Apple fees.
  - A2 before any launch: most buyers aren't chess players; without onboarding the
    spectacle never lands and you get "I don't know what I'm doing" refunds.
  - Steam (B) is where the money is, but it's the $100 + native-build tier — do it
    once itch has validated interest and copy.
  - C1 (Story) is the cheapest way to stop being "a Battle Chess clone" and become
    "that orchestra chess game with the campaign" — high perceived value, mostly
    text + reuse of existing scenes.
  - Mobile (D) is last: biggest audience but the heaviest packaging/review friction
    and the existing v1.4 mobile support still needs the V2 real-device playtest.
